The Bonn Agreement is an international agreement by North Sea coastal states, together with the EC to: offer mutual assistance and co-operation in combating pollution; execute surveillance as an aid to detecting and combating pollution and to prevent violations of anti-pollution regulations.   The Bonn Agreement is a network of professionals with responsibility for adequate pollution response. The members of the Bonn Agreement are Belgium, Denmark, European Community, France, Germany, the Netherlands, Norway, Sweden, the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland. Ireland will join the Bonn Agreement shortly. Representatives of these authorities ensure the execution of the Agreement. Many European countries are leaders in the field of surveillance and combating marine pollution. The members of the Bonn Agreement utilise this expertise with the plans and management of pollution incidents.
